Key Insights

Seasonal Patterns

Temperature exhibits clear seasonal variation with summer averages reaching 30°C and winter averages dropping to 10°C. This seasonal trend directly influences solar energy availability throughout the year.

Solar Irradiance

Solar irradiance remains zero during night hours and peaks during daytime, reaching maximum values near 1000 W/m². Peak generation occurs between 12 PM and 2 PM daily.

Cloud Cover Impact

High cloud cover above 80% significantly reduces solar irradiance to an average of 17 W/m². Cloud conditions are a critical factor affecting solar energy availability and system performance.

Clear Sky Conditions

Low cloud cover below 20% results in higher solar irradiance averaging 237 W/m². Clear sky conditions provide optimal circumstances for maximum solar energy capture and generation.

Summary

Key Outcomes

  • Seasonal patterns demonstrate clear temperature variations with summer averages reaching 30°C and winter averages dropping to 10°C, directly influencing solar energy availability throughout the year
  • Solar irradiance exhibits distinct day-night behavior with zero values during night hours and peak generation between 12 PM and 2 PM, reaching maximum values near 1000 W/m²
  • Cloud cover significantly impacts solar availability, with high cloud conditions (above 80%) reducing irradiance to 17 W/m² while clear sky conditions (below 20% cloud cover) enable optimal generation at 237 W/m²
  • Heatmap analysis reveals consistent solar generation patterns across months with peak intensity during fall and summer seasons
